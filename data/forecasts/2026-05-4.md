## Overview of the Situation

An Enhanced Risk (ENH) is in place across parts of Germany and Czechia, surrounded by a broader Slight Risk (SLGT) extending further across both countries, and an even larger Marginal Risk (MRGL) reaching into Switzerland and southwestern Germany. Thunderstorms are expected to initiate around midday, initially as scattered cells, before organizing and growing upscale through the afternoon and evening. By nighttime, there is a strong signal for large mesoscale convective systems (MCS) developing and propagating across the region. While radar trends suggest potentially intense storm structures, the relatively limited instability introduces uncertainty, meaning the event could either produce organized severe storms or evolve into a more widespread stratiform rain shield with embedded convection.

## Synopsis

Within the ENH area (Germany and Czechia), soundings indicate modest instability, with CAPE generally around 500–1000 J/kg, locally slightly higher where heating is maximized. Despite this not being particularly high, the environment compensates with strong wind shear, with deep-layer shear likely exceeding 30–40 kt. This combination favors organized convection, including supercells early on and a transition into linear systems or MCS structures later. Hodographs show notable curvature and length, supporting storm organization and rotation potential, especially during the early convective phase. Mid-level lapse rates are moderate, enough to sustain updrafts but not extreme, which limits very large hail potential.

In the surrounding SLGT area, particularly across Germany and extending further into Czechia, instability becomes slightly weaker (around 400–800 J/kg CAPE), but shear remains supportive of organized storms. This suggests a continuation of multicell clusters and line segments, with storms likely merging into larger complexes. The main hazards here include strong to severe wind gusts and heavy rainfall, with hail being more limited compared to higher-CAPE setups.

Across the broader MRGL area, including Switzerland and southwestern Germany, soundings show low instability (generally below ~500 J/kg CAPE) and weaker lapse rates, but still sufficient moisture for widespread precipitation with embedded convection. Shear remains present, which could allow low-topped organized showers or weak storms, but severe potential is limited.

A key uncertainty in this setup is the relatively low CAPE versus strong shear environment. While shear strongly supports storm organization and MCS development, the limited instability may cause storms to quickly transition into a large stratiform system with embedded convection, rather than maintaining widespread severe intensity. Nonetheless, the potential for organized evening convection and nocturnal MCS development remains significant, especially within the ENH corridor.
