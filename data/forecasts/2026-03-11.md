## Overview of the Situation

Two Marginal Risk (MRGL) areas have been outlined across parts of Germany, Czechia, and a small region near Vienna in Austria. Overall thunderstorm chances remain limited, but the first MRGL area covering Germany and parts of Czechia has a slightly higher probability of isolated thunderstorms compared to surrounding regions. In this zone, scattered showers with occasional lightning may develop as modest instability overlaps with weak forcing.

A second smaller MRGL area includes a small region around Vienna in Austria and a localized area in Czechia, where the probability of thunderstorms is very low. These areas may see mostly showers, with only a minimal chance of embedded thunder if localized instability briefly increases. Overall, storm coverage will be very isolated, and most locations will likely experience no thunderstorms at all.

## Synopsis

In the bigger MRGL area, soundings from Germany and Czechia indicate weak but noticeable instability, with CAPE generally around 200–400 J/kg. The thermodynamic profile shows a moist lower atmosphere and somewhat steeper low- to mid-level lapse rates, which could allow low-topped convection to develop. Wind profiles show weak to modest deep-layer shear (around 15–20 kt), which is insufficient for organized storms but adequate for brief, weak convective cells. As a result, isolated thunderstorms embedded within showers are possible, mainly producing brief heavy rain and occasional lightning rather than severe weather.

In the smaller MRGL area, including Vienna in Austria and a small part of Czechia, the soundings show much weaker instability, with CAPE generally below ~200 J/kg and relatively shallow convective depth. The vertical profiles are more stable and saturated, suggesting that most precipitation will occur as stratiform or weak convective showers. Wind shear is also very weak, further limiting any chance of storm organization. While a very isolated lightning strike cannot be ruled out, the environment strongly favors non-thunderstorm showers, which is why the thunderstorm probability in this area remains very small.
