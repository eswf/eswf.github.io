## Overview of the Situation

A Marginal Risk (MRGL) is in place across western Czechia and parts of Germany, where the dominant weather mode will be rain showers, with a low-end chance of embedded thunderstorms. Convective activity is expected to be isolated and poorly organized, with most locations experiencing only stratiform or weakly convective precipitation. However, localized conditions may briefly support low-topped thunderstorms, especially during periods of enhanced lift. Overall, severe weather is not expected, and any lightning activity will remain sporadic and short-lived.

## Synopsis

Soundings from western Czechia and Germany show a cool and moist vertical profile, with limited instability, generally featuring CAPE values below ~300 J/kg. The lapse rates are relatively weak to moderate, especially in the mid-levels, which limits strong updraft development. However, there is enough low-level moisture and weak surface heating to allow for shallow convective elements embedded within broader rain areas.

Wind profiles indicate weak shear throughout the column (generally below 15–20 kt), meaning any convection that develops will remain disorganized and short-lived. The hodographs are mostly straight and small, further supporting a lack of storm organization.

In both regions, the atmosphere appears nearly saturated through a deep layer, favoring persistent showers and occasional heavier bursts of rain. Any thunderstorms that do form will likely be low-topped, producing brief lightning and locally enhanced rainfall, but lacking the structure needed for severe weather.

Overall, the setup strongly favors widespread rain showers with only a marginal chance of thunder, consistent with the MRGL classification.
