## Overview of the Situation

Two Slight Risk (SLGT) areas are forecast across Central Europe. The primary and larger SLGT area extends across Germany, Czechia, and into Poland, where thunderstorms are expected to initiate mainly over Germany and Czechia and then propagate eastward into Poland. A second, smaller SLGT area is present over parts of Switzerland and adjacent regions near the Italy border, as well as a small portion of southern Germany. Surrounding both SLGT areas is a broad Marginal Risk (MRGL) covering Germany, Austria, Czechia, and Poland, where isolated thunderstorms or showers may occur, though with lower confidence and intensity. Overall, the setup favors scattered thunderstorms, with the greatest coverage and organization in the main SLGT area.

## Synopsis

Within the primary SLGT area, soundings across Germany and Czechia show moderate instability, with CAPE generally around 800–1400 J/kg, locally higher where surface heating is maximized. Mid-level lapse rates are moderately steep (~6–7 K/km), supporting robust updraft development. Wind profiles indicate moderate deep-layer shear (20–30 kt), sufficient for organized multicells and possibly transient supercells, especially in Germany. Hodographs show some curvature, suggesting a low-end potential for storm organization, though not strongly supportive of long-lived supercells.

As storms move into Poland, soundings show slightly reduced instability and somewhat weaker lapse rates, but still enough CAPE to maintain organized convection, particularly in the form of clusters or line segments. This supports the idea that storms will primarily travel into Poland rather than initiate there, bringing gusty winds, small to marginal hail, and heavy rain.

In the secondary SLGT area over Switzerland, soundings indicate modest CAPE (~500–1000 J/kg) with relatively moist profiles and weaker shear compared to the main SLGT zone. This suggests scattered pulse storms or short-lived multicells, mainly producing heavy rainfall and occasional lightning. Near the Switzerland–Italy border, despite similar thermodynamics, model guidance shows limited convective initiation, likely due to weak forcing or capping influences, meaning storms may remain sparse.

The surrounding MRGL area features lower instability (generally 300–800 J/kg CAPE) and weaker shear, favoring isolated and disorganized convection. In Austria, soundings actually suggest slightly better instability and moisture, but the lack of strong model support introduces uncertainty, meaning storms may remain isolated despite a somewhat favorable environment. Across Germany, Czechia, and Poland, MRGL soundings show moist, weakly unstable profiles, supporting mainly showers with occasional thunder.

Overall, the environment supports scattered thunderstorms with moderate organization in the main SLGT area, transitioning into weaker and more isolated convection outward into the MRGL zone.
