## Overview of the Situation

A Slight Risk (SLGT) is forecast across parts of Germany, Czechia, Slovenia, and a small portion of Switzerland, where scattered thunderstorms are expected. Storms may either initiate locally (especially over Germany and Czechia) or move into the region from surrounding areas, particularly into Switzerland from the south. Activity will likely remain somewhat scattered, but conditions support organized multicells and localized stronger storms. A surrounding Marginal Risk (MRGL) extends into Austria, additional parts of Germany, and Czechia, where isolated thunderstorms may still develop, though with lower coverage and intensity.

## Synopsis

Across the SLGT area, soundings in Germany and Czechia indicate moderate instability, with CAPE generally around 600–1200 J/kg, locally higher in pockets where moisture and weak heating overlap. Mid-level lapse rates are moderate, sufficient for sustained updrafts, though not particularly steep. Wind profiles show moderate deep-layer shear (~20–30 kt), supporting organized multicells and possibly brief supercell structures, especially where storms remain discrete early on. Hodographs display some curvature, suggesting limited but present storm-scale rotation potential.

In Slovenia, the environment appears more moist and somewhat unstable, with similar CAPE values but slightly weaker shear, favoring pulse to multicell convection, with the main threats being heavy rainfall and localized gusty winds.
For Switzerland, soundings suggest storms will likely be elevated or advective, moving in from Italy, rather than initiating locally. The profiles show adequate moisture and weak to moderate instability, allowing storms to persist, but with limited intensification, favoring rain-heavy convection with occasional lightning.

In the surrounding MRGL area, including Austria, Germany, and Czechia, instability decreases to around 300–700 J/kg CAPE, and shear remains modest or weak. This supports isolated, short-lived storms, often embedded within broader shower activity.

Overall, the setup favors scattered thunderstorms with moderate organization in the SLGT zone, gradually transitioning into isolated and weaker convection toward the MRGL area, with the main hazards being heavy rain, gusty winds, and occasional small hail.
